Via Appia (Romans): The "Queen of Roads" - Rome's first great highway, representing the gospel's advance to the heart of empire.
Via Egnatia (1 Corinthians): The east-west highway of commerce, representing the intersection of cultures and the challenge of faithful living in cosmopolitan settings.
Via Sebaste (2 Corinthians): The mountain road through difficult terrain, representing ministry's hardships and the weakness through which God's strength is perfected.
Via Maris (Galatians): The ancient "Way of the Sea," representing freedom from bondage and the universal scope of the gospel.
Via Traiana (Ephesians): Trajan's masterpiece of engineering, representing unity and the bringing together of diverse peoples under one authority.
Via Egnatia Eastern (Philippians): The port road where Paul first landed in Europe, representing new beginnings and gospel partnership.
